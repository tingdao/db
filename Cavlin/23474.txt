<div class="comment-content"><p><strong>14.</strong><em>And if you are willing to receive it </em>He now explains more clearly in what manner John began to preach <em>the kingdom of God </em>It was in the character of that <em>Elijah, </em>who was to be sent before the face of God, (<span class="bvs">Mal_4:5</span>.) Our Lord’s meaning therefore is, that <em>the great and dreadful day of the Lord, </em>which Malachi described, is now beheld by the Jews, when <em>Elijah, </em>who was there promised, discharges his office as a herald. Again, by this exception, <em>if you are willing to receive it, </em>he glances at their hardened obstinacy, in maliciously shutting their eyes against the clearest light. But will he cease to be <em>Elijah, </em>if he shall not be <em>received? </em>Christ does not mean that John’s official <em>character </em><span class="cite-ref">(17)</span> depends on their approbation; but having declared that he is <em>Elijah, </em>he charges them with carelessness and ingratitude, if he does not obtain that respect to which he is entitled. </p><div class="notes"><p><span class="cite-note">(17)</span> “<span class="lang fr-fr">L’estat et la commission de Iean</span>;” — “John’s rank and commission.” </p></div></div>

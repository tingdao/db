<div class="comment-content"><p><strong>20</strong><em>Now to our God and Father </em>This may be taken as a general thanksgiving, by which he closes the epistle; or it may be viewed as bearing more particularly upon the last clause in reference to the liberality shewn to Paul. <span class="cite-ref">(259)</span> For in respect of the assistance which the Philippians had afforded him, it became him to reckon himself indebted to them for it in such a manner as to acknowledge, that this aid had been afforded to them by the mercy of God. </p><div class="notes"><p><span class="cite-note">(259)</span> “<span class="lang fr-fr">La liberalite de laquelle les Philippiens auoyent vse enuers sainct Paul</span>;” — “The liberality which the Philippians had exercised towards St. Paul.” </p></div></div>

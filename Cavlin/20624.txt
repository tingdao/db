<div class="comment-content"><p>Now the manner of that vengeance which was lately mentioned is expressed. Hence the Prophet says, God exclaimed, so that his command reached to the Chaldeans, who were to be executors of his vengeance, and therefore the imperative mood pleases me better, <em>approach ye therefore. </em>Those who consider the tense past say “visitations,” nor can they do otherwise, because no sense can be elicited from the words — to have approached the prefecture of the city. But if we read the imperative mood, the sense agrees very well, <em>approach ye the prefecture: </em>the thing is put for the persons, or the name of the men may be understood, and thus <span class="lang he-il">פקדות</span>, <em>phekdoth, </em>may be taken in the genitive <em>case. </em>As to the general meaning, God commands his servants who held authority over the devoted city, <em>to approach, </em>or apply themselves, or be ready to fulfill his work, and <em>let each, </em>says he, <em>have his instrument of destruction: </em>here destruction is taken actively. For God does not mean that the Chaldeans were armed for their own destruction, but for that of the Jews, and the ruin of the city. It follows — </p></div>

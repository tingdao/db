<div class="comment-content"><p><strong>13.</strong><em>Wherefore let him that speaketh in another tongue </em>This is an anticipation, by way of reply to a question which might very readily be proposed to him. <em>“If </em>any one, therefore, is able to speak a foreign language, will the gift be useless? Why should that be kept back, which might be brought out to light, to the glory of God?” He shows the remedy. <em>“Let </em>him,” says he, <em>“ask </em>from God the gift of interpretation also. If he is without this, let him abstain in the meantime from ostentation.” <span class="cite-ref">(826)</span></p><div class="notes"><p><span class="cite-note">(826)</span> “<span class="lang fr-fr">De parler a ostentation</span>;” — “From speaking for ostentation’s sake.” </p></div></div>

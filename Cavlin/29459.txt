<div class="comment-content"><p><strong>17.</strong><em>Not that I demand a gift. </em>Again he repels an unfavourable opinion that might be formed of immoderate cupidity, that they might not suppose that it was an indirect hint, <span class="cite-ref">(252)</span> as if they ought singly to stand in the room of all, <span class="cite-ref">(253)</span> and as if he abused their kindness. He accordingly declares, that he consulted not so much his own advantage as theirs. “While I receive from you,” says he, <em>“there </em>is proportionably much advantage that redounds to yourselves; for there are just so many articles that you may reckon to have been transferred to the table of accounts.” The meaning of this word <span class="cite-ref">(254)</span> is connected with the similitude formerly employed of exchange or compensation in pecuniary matters. </p><div class="notes"><p><span class="cite-note">(252)</span> “<em class="lang fr-fr">Pour </em><span class="lang fr-fr">les induire a continuer</span>;” — “To induce them to hold on.” </p><p><span class="cite-note">(253)</span> “<span class="lang fr-fr">Comme si eux deussent tenir la place de tous, et faire pour les autres</span>;” — “As if they ought to hold the place of all, and to act in the room of others.” </p><p><span class="cite-note">(254)</span> Calvin evidently refers to the word <span class="lang el-gr">λόγον</span>, <em>(account,) </em>which the Apostle had made use of in <span class="bvs">Phi_4:15</span>, in the phrase <span class="lang el-gr">εἰς λόγον δόσεω; καὶ λήψεω;</span>, <em>(in the matter of giving and receiving.) </em>It is noticed by <em>Beza, </em>that the Rabbins make use of a corresponding phrase אשמו ןחמ <em>(mattan umassa) </em>— <em>giving and taking. </em>— <em>Ed. </em></p></div></div>

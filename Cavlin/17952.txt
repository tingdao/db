<div class="comment-content"><p><strong>23.</strong><em>And I will make it to be a possession of the hedgehog. </em><span class="cite-ref">(228)</span> He again confirms the same things which he formerly predicted, namely, that henceforth it will not be a habitation of men, but will resemble a hideous cavern, in which wild beasts shall lurk. <span class="lang he-il">קפד</span> (<em>kippod</em>) is rendered by some <em>a beaver</em>, by some <em>a tortoise</em>, and by others <em>a hedgehog</em>. From the connection of the passage, it is probable that the Prophet spoke of an animal that is found near the water; for he afterwards mentions <em>pools of water</em>. This applies strictly to the situation of the place, for though Babylon did not lie in a marsh, yet it lay in a moist place, the country around it being watered on one side by the Euphrates, and on the other by the Tigris. Hence the Lord threatens to bring a deluge upon it. <span class="cite-ref">(229)</span></p><div class="notes"><p><span class="cite-note">(228)</span> Bogus footnote </p><p><span class="cite-note">(229)</span> Bogus footnote </p></div></div>

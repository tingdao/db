<div class="comment-content"><p><strong>19.</strong><em>For ye bear with fools willingly. </em>He calls them <em>wise </em>— in my opinion, <em>ironically. </em>He was despised by them, which could not have been, had they not been puffed up with the greatest arrogance <span class="cite-ref">(843)</span> He says, therefore — “Since you are so wise, act the part of wise men in bearing with me, whom you treat with contempt, as you would a <em>fool.” </em>Hence I infer, that this discourse is not addressed to all indiscriminately, but some particular persons are reproved, who conducted themselves in an unkind manner. <span class="cite-ref">(844)</span></p><div class="notes"><p><span class="cite-note">(843)</span><span class="cite-ref">“</span><span class="lang fr-fr">D’vne merueilleuse arrogance</span>;” — “With an amazing arrogance.” </p><p><span class="cite-note">(844)</span> “<span class="lang fr-fr">Enuers luy</span>;” — “Towards him.” </p></div></div>

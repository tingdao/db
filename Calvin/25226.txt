<div class="comment-content"><p><strong>30.</strong><em>Despised the counsel of God within themselves. The counsel of God </em>is mentioned by way of respect, as contrasted with the wicked pride of the scribes; for the term <em>counsel </em>carries along with it a dignity, which protects the doctrine of God against the contempt of men. Literally, Luke says, that they <em>despised </em>Against Themselves : and indeed I do not disapprove of the meaning which is preferred by some, that the scribes were rebellious to their own destruction. But as Luke’s narrative is simple, and as the preposition <span class="lang el-gr">εἰς</span> is often used in the sense of <span class="lang el-gr">ἐν</span> I have chosen rather to translate it, <em>within themselves; </em>as meaning, that although they did not openly and expressly contradict, yet as they inwardly swelled with hidden pride, they <em>despised within themselves </em></p></div>

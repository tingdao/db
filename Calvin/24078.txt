<div class="comment-content"><p><strong>23.</strong><em>But he answering said. </em>Christ, by his reply, neither removes their doubt, nor points out the person of Judas, but only confirms what he said a little before, that <em>one of his friends sitting at the table </em>is the traitor. And though they thought it hard to be left in suspense and perplexity for a time, that they might employ themselves in contemplating the atrocity of the crime, it was afterwards followed by another advantage, when they perceived that the prediction of the psalm was fulfilled, </p><p>He that ate pleasant food with me <span class="cite-ref">(184)</span><LineBreak />hath lifted up his heel against me, (<span class="bvs">Psa_41:10</span>.) </p><p>Besides, in the person of Judas, our Lord intended to admonish his followers in all ages, not to be discouraged or faint on account of intimate friends proving to be traitors; because the same thing that was experienced by Him who is the Head of the whole Church, must happen to us who are members of it. </p><div class="notes"><p><span class="cite-note">(184)</span> “<em class="lang fr-fr">Celuy qui mangeoit en ami avec moy </em><em>; </em>” — “<em>he that ate with me as a friend. </em>” </p></div></div>

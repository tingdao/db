<div class="comment-content"><p><strong>7.</strong><em>Peace be within thy bulwarks, </em>etc. The two clauses express the same sentiment, and, therefore, the meaning of the first is gathered from the second. The term <em>peace </em>signifies nothing else than prosperity. The noun <span class="lang he-il">שלוה</span>, <em>shalvah, </em>in the second clause, sometimes signifies <em>rest, </em>but it is more frequently taken for <em>abundance </em>or <em>prosperity </em>On this account I have translated the noun <span class="lang he-il">בחילך</span><em>, bechelech, within thy bulwark </em><span class="cite-ref">(73)</span> I do not find fault with others who have translated it <em>a ditch </em>or <em>outward wall; </em>but the word <em>bulwark </em>agrees better with the word <em>towers, </em>which occurs at the close of the verse. The amount is, that David prays for the prosperity of the Church through its whole extent. Moreover, it is to be noticed, that when he offers supplication for its external prosperity, it is not to be understood as implying that he was unconcerned about its internal state or spiritual well being; but under the similitude of <em>walls, </em><span class="cite-ref">(74)</span> he wishes that on all sides the blessing of God may environ and fortify the holy city. </p><div class="notes"><p><span class="cite-note">(73)</span> Calvin’s meaning is, that as the nouns <em>peace </em>and <em>prosperity </em>have a corresponding signification, he was of opinion, that there existed a similar correspondence between the other two nouns. </p><p><span class="cite-note">(74)</span> The Latin copy here reads, “sed ad mores alludens;” but <em>mores </em> is evidently a typographical error for <em>muros </em> The French version has “<span class="lang fr-fr">mais sous ceste similitude </span><em class="lang fr-fr">des murs </em> ” </p></div></div>

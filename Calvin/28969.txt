<div class="comment-content"><p><strong>13.</strong><em>By the experiment of that administration </em>The term <em>experiment </em>here, as in a variety of other places, means <em>proof </em>or <em>trial </em><span class="cite-ref">(733)</span> For it was a sufficient token for bringing the love of the Corinthians to the test, — that they were so liberal to brethren that were at a great distance from them. Paul, however, extends it farther — to their concurrent obedience in the gospel. <span class="cite-ref">(734)</span> For by such proofs we truly manifest, that we are obedient to the doctrine of the gospel. Now their concurrence appears from this — that alms are conferred with the common consent of all. </p><div class="notes"><p><span class="cite-note">(733)</span> “<span class="lang fr-fr">Tesmoignage, enseignement, ou experience</span>;” — “Proof, voucher, or trial.” </p><p><span class="cite-note">(734)</span> “<span class="lang fr-fr">Leur obeissance qu’ils rendoyent tons d’vn accord a l’euangile</span>;” — “Their obedience which they rendered, all with one accord, to the gospel.” </p></div></div>

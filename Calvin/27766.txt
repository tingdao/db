<div class="comment-content"><p><strong>32.</strong><em>And the next day. </em>Though Luke did not express before that the soldiers were commanded to return before they came at their way’s end, yet it is certain, that they were appointed to accompany him only unto that place where the chief captain thought Paul would be safe; for he went out privily in the night. And the chief captain knew that so soon as they had finished some part of their journey, there was no farther danger, because the adversaries could have no hope to overtake him; and that it was no point of wisdom<span class="cite-ref">(561)</span> to send part of the garrison far away. </p><p><strong>(561)</strong></p><p>“ Tutum... non esse,” it was not safe. </p></div>

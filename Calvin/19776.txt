<div class="comment-content"><p>To shew more fully what is said in the preceding verse, he adds, <em>Fields with money shall be bought, and by writing </em>(the verbs are in the infinitive mood) they <em>shall write on tablets and sign by witnesses </em>even <em>if the land of Benjamin </em><span class="cite-ref">(85)</span> Then the Prophet mentions all the boundaries by which Jerusalem was surrounded. We know that a part of the city was in the lot of Benjamin, and even one gate was so called: <em>in the land of Benjamin, </em>he says, and also <em>through the circuits of Jerusalem, even in the cities of Judah, </em>those on the <em>mountains, </em>as well as those <em>in the valley, and in the cities </em>which he to <em>the south, </em>even Egypt, for the southern country was towards Egypt. The reason is added, <em>Because God </em>would <em>restore </em>their <em>captivity, </em>that, is, restore the captives that they might again rossess the land. Now follows, — </p><p><strong>(85)</strong> The infinitives in Hebrew are often as in <em>Welsh, </em>verbal nouns. The rendering may be made as follows, — </p><p>Fields with money shall they buy; And <em>there shall be </em>writing in a book, And sealing, and the witnessing of witnesses, In the land of Benjamin, etc. etc. </p><p>Our version is the <em>Syr. </em>and nearly the <em>Targ. </em>— <em>Ed. </em></p></div>

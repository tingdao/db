<div class="comment-content"><p><strong>11.</strong><em>Whether I or they </em>Having compared himself with the other Apostles, he now associates himself with them, and them with him, in agreement as to their preaching. “I do not now speak of myself, but we have all taught so with one mouth, and still continue to teach so.” For the verb <span class="lang el-gr">κηρύσσομεν</span><em>(we preach) </em>is in the present tense — intimating a continued act, or perseverance in teaching. <span class="cite-ref">(31)</span> “If, then, it is otherwise, our apostleship is void: nay more — <em>so ye believed: </em>your religion, therefore, goes for nothing.” </p><div class="notes"><p><span class="cite-note">(31)</span> “<span class="lang fr-fr">Perseuerance a enseigner ceste mesme chose</span>;” — “Perseverance in teaching this same thing.” </p></div></div>

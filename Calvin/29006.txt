<div class="comment-content"><p><strong>17.</strong><em>What I speak, I speak not after the Lord </em>His disposition, it is true, had an eye to God, but the outward appearance <span class="cite-ref">(838)</span> might seem unsuitable to a servant of the Lord. At the same time, the things that Paul confesses respecting himself, he, on the other hand, condemns in the false Apostles. <span class="cite-ref">(839)</span> For it was not his intention to praise himself, but simply to contrast himself with them, with the view of humbling them. <span class="cite-ref">(840)</span> Hence he transfers to his own person what belonged to them, that he may thus open the eyes of the Corinthians. What I have rendered <em>boldness, </em>is in the Greek <span class="lang el-gr">ὑπόστασις</span><em>, </em>as to the meaning of which term we have spoken in the ninth chapter. (<span class="bvs">2Co_9:4</span>.) <em>Subject-matter </em><span class="cite-ref">(841)</span> or <em>substance, </em>unquestionably, would not be at all suitable here. <span class="cite-ref">(842)</span></p><div class="notes"><p><span class="cite-note">(838)</span> “<span class="lang fr-fr">La facon exterieure en laquelle il procede</span>;” — “The outward manner in which he goes to work.” </p><p><span class="cite-note">(839)</span> “<span class="lang fr-fr">C’est plustos afin de lea condemner es faux-Apostres</span>;” — “It is rather with the view of condemning them in the false Apostles.” </p><p><span class="cite-note">(840)</span><span class="cite-ref">“</span><span class="lang fr-fr">Afin de leur abbaisser le coquet</span> :” — “With the view of bringing down their talk.” </p><p><span class="cite-note">(841)</span> Calvin refers here to the rendering of Erasmus, and of the Vulgate. The term employed by Erasmus is <em>argumenturm </em> (<em>subject-matter.</em>) In accordance with this, Cranmer’s version (1539) reads, “in this <em>matter </em>of boastinge.” The Vulgate makes use of the term <em>substantia </em><em>, </em>(<em>substance.</em>) Wiclif (1380) reads, “in this substance of glorie” The Rheims version (1582), “in this substance of glorying.” — <em>Ed</em>. </p><p><span class="cite-note">(842)</span><span class="cite-ref">“</span><span class="lang fr-fr">Certes il ne conueniendroit pas bien yci de traduire </span><em class="lang fr-fr">matiere </em><span class="lang fr-fr">ou </span><em class="lang fr-fr">substance, </em><span class="lang fr-fr">combien que le mot signifie quelque fois cela</span>;” — “Certainly it would not be suitable here to render it <em>subject-matter </em>or <em>substance, </em>though the word sometimes bears that meaning.” </p></div></div>

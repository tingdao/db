<div class="comment-content"><p><strong>2.</strong><em>And if I should have the gift of prophecy. </em>He brings down to nothing the dignity of even this endowment, <span class="cite-ref">(777)</span> which, nevertheless, he had preferred to all others. <em>To know all mysteries, </em>might seem to be added to the term <em>prophecy, </em>by way of explanation, but as the term <em>knowledge </em>is immediately added, of which he had previously made mention by itself, (<span class="bvs">1Co_12:8</span>,) it will deserve your consideration, whether the <em>knowledge of mysteries </em>may not be used here to mean <em>wisdom. </em>As for myself, while I would not venture to affirm that it is so, I am much inclined to that opinion. </p><p>That <em>faith, </em>of which he speaks, is special, as is evident from the clause that is immediately added — so <em>that I remove mountains </em>Hence the Sophists accomplish nothing, when they pervert this passage for the purpose of detracting from the excellence of faith. As, therefore, the term <em>faith </em>is <em>( </em><span class="lang el-gr">πολύσημον</span><em>) used in a variety of senses, </em>it is the part of the prudent reader to observe in what signification it is taken. Paul, however, as I have already stated, is his own interpreter, by restricting <em>faith, </em>here, to miracles. It is what Chrysostom calls the <em>“faith </em>of miracles,” and what we term a “special faith,” because it does not apprehend a whole Christ, but simply his power in working miracles; and hence it may sometimes exist in a man without the Spirit of sanctification, as it did in Judas. <span class="cite-ref">(778)</span></p><div class="notes"><p><span class="cite-note">(777)</span> “<span class="lang fr-fr">La dignite mesme de la prophetie</span>;” — “The dignity even of prophecy.” </p><p><span class="cite-note">(778)</span> The reader will observe, that this is, in substance, what has been stated by Calvin previously, when commenting on <span class="bvs">1Co_12:10</span>. — <em>Ed</em>. </p></div></div>

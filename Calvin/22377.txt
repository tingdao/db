<div class="comment-content"><p>He says in the last place, <em>I will send fire on Teman, to consume the palaces of Bozrah </em>By fire he ever means any kind of destruction. But he compares God’s vengeance to a burning fire. We know that when fire has once taken hold, not only on a house, but on a whole city, there is no remedy. So now the Prophet says, that God’s vengeance would be dreadful, that it would consume whatever hatred there was among them:<em>I will </em>then <em>send fire on Teman</em>; which, as it is well known, was the first city of Idumea.
